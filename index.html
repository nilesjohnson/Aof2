Instructions

Clicking a dot displays the information about it which you have selected from the menu at right. 'Adem' or 'Milnor' shows the representation of the element in either the admissable basis or the Milnor basis, respectively. 'None' will turn this display off.

The 'Operation' menu selects the subset of operations which will be displayed. For example, if all three operations, \(Sq^1\), \(Sq^2\), and \(Sq^4\), are all selected, then clicking on the dot corresponding to \(x\) will cause \(Sq^1 x\), \(Sq^2 x\), and \(Sq^4 x\), to be highlighted, in the colors corresponding to the menu entries. If one of these values is zero, the dot in the upper left labelled zero will be highlighted to indicate that. If the result is a sum, all terms in the sum will be highlighted.

Explanation of the display

It is (just barely) possible to represent \(\mathcal{A}(2)\) by drawing dots representing a basis for it, together with lines representing the left action of \(Sq^1\), \(Sq^2\), and \(Sq^4\) (see Andre Henriques' drawing).

The representation here breaks \(\mathcal{A}(2)\) into the 8 left cosets of \(\mathcal{A}(1)\). This has several useful features:

  • It is 'even-handed', giving 8 subsets of size 8.
  • It is well related to \(ko\)-theory.
  • It has a simple form, since \(\mathcal{A}(2)/\!/\mathcal{A}(1) = \mathcal{A}(2)/\mathcal{A}(2)(Sq^1,Sq^2)\) is concentrated in dimensions 0, 4, 6, 7, 10, 11, 13 and 17.
These cosets are represented by
  • \( 1\)
  • \( Sq^4\)
  • \( Sq^2 Sq^4\)
  • \( Sq^3 Sq^4 \)
  • \( Sq^4 Sq^2 Sq^4\)
  • \( Sq^5 Sq^2 Sq^4 = Sq^4 Sq^3 Sq^4 \)
  • \( Sq^2 Sq^4 Sq^3 Sq^4\)
  • \( Sq^4 Sq^2 Sq^4 Sq^3 Sq^4 \)
which can be seen across the top of the display. The copy of \(\mathcal{A}(1)\) 'hanging' from \(x\) contains all the classes in \(x\mathcal{A}(1)\) where \(x\) is one of these 8 elements.

This gives a description of each element in terms of the generators \(Sq^1\), \(Sq^2\), and \(Sq^4\) in the form \(xy\), where \(x\) is one of the 8 elements above, selecting the coset, and \(y\) is an element of \(\mathcal{A}(1)\). For example, the third element from the top in the third group from the left has the representation \((Sq^2 Sq^4)(Sq^2)\). In the admissable basis, this is \(Sq^6 Sq^2\), which can be verified by clicking on it, while the 'Adem' menu item is on.

Thus, the cosets are given by left multiplications, while the elements within the coset are given by right multiplications. In this form, the left action of \(\mathcal{A}(2)\) on itself is not readily visible. The 'Operation' menu is there to provide this information. Some of the actions will be obvious, such as \(Sq^2\) acting on the second or sixth \(\mathcal{A}(1)\) or \(Sq^4\) acting on the first or next to last \(\mathcal{A}(1)\). The values \(Sq^i x\) are the sums of the highlighted items, if nonzero. If the result is zero, the corresponding dot in the upper left, labelled zero, is highlighted.
